The University campus

City, University of London's Northampton Square campus is located in the heart of London, near the historic Clerkenwell area.

The campus is easily accessible by public transportation, with several tube stations and bus stops in the vicinity. It houses various academic departments, lecture theaters, laboratories, libraries, and other facilities to support students in their studies.

As a City University student, you will have many opportunities within our campus, as there are many spaces to visit. Students have the following spaces to explore :

  • Angel, Islington - A lively part of town known for its restaurants, independent boutiques and theatres. There's always fun to be had any time of day or night.
  • Trendy Shoreditch - Full of creative spaces and vintage shops, not to mention iconic street art and the famous Columbia Road flower market.
  • The Museum of London - Everything you need to know about London under one roof. It also houses the London 2012 Olympic Cauldron.
  • Clerkenwell - Home for history museums and a hub for creative start-ups. This trendy area where Charles Dickens once lived also has some of the city's oldest pubs.
  • The Students' Union supports social, political and cultural clubs and societies - Aids students to set up new societies.
  • CityBar - A great place to eat, socialise or relax. At night, it holds society events, DJ sets and themed parties.
  • CitySport - Our student sports centre, for classes, court sports and a fully-equipped gym.

City Student Life

As a City student, you will be expected to attend your lectures, tutorials, lab session and much more at our campus. We focus on ensuring that all of our students are comfortable in our campus, and that all students are able to achieve what they want and also succeed. We have all the facilities a student could ever need, and we are always looking for ways to improve our campus. We have a wide range of activities and clubs that students can participate, as well as a wide range of events that students can attend. Our students will also be free to use all available spaces to students around our campus.

As a City Student, you will gain a lot of support regarding your future, as we have our own employability section within our campus where you can visit and book appointments to discuss about your future plans, what you intend on doing after your course and what you want to achieve, for which we deliver the most useful resource.

We care about our students mental health, which is why we have set up a section within our Northampton Square Campus where student can book appointment with a member of staff working within that section to discuss about their problems.

As a City Student, you will also have access to many resources for your studies and module with our 5 floor library and also virtual library where students can access essential books which can aid their studies. We want our students to be successful, and in order for that to happen, we have many available resources out there for our students to utilise for their needs. Within our In-Campus library, there are many spaces for individual and group studies, as we have bookable private rooms for students to book and have meetings, work together and complete assignments together. Our latest construction work in our library finally finished, which is our 5th Floor dedicated to silent study.

Our students will not need to worry about missing prayers, as we have prayer rooms seperate for both genders, making it easier for our students to also be close to religion whilst maintaining their education.

Current City Students studying CS Foundation Year

Our current foundation year students recommend choosing this programme, as they are benefitign from doing the programme themselves. Our Foundation students are able to experience a variety of things such as the Universwity lifestyle, University freedom and individual study, what University studies are like and most of all gaining relevant and essential information and skills relavent to the field of the programme.

Our Current students belive that what they are leaning from their lectures, tutroials and lab sessions are productive and useful, which they havent been taught previously and feel that they are learning and developmig skills which they will benefit from when landing a job in the field of Computer Science, or even when studying for a full degree which would set foundation students a step above.

After conducting researches, we have gathered opinions which concludes that foundation year in Computer Science really developed a key foundation on Programming and Mathematical side of CS, and that they do belive they have problem solving and critical thinking skills developed only because they have chose to do foundation year.
